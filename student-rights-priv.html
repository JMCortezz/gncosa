STUDENT RIGHTS AND PRIVILEGES

Students’ rights and freedom are to be exercised with responsibility and prudence. These rights and freedom should not be used by any student as a means to discredit the College and its members or to prevent it from performing its normal conduct of school activities.

The following rights and privileges shall be enjoyed by any bona fide student of the College:

1.1 Every student enjoys freedom of organization in accordance with the laws and school policies.

1.2 Every student is encouraged to join college club/organization.

1.3 Organizations espousing ideas or policies inimical to the rights and interests of any individual, group or the entire college, are prohibited on campus.

2.1 Every student enjoys freedom of expression, speech and of the press; bearing in mind that freedom of expression is not absolute. Any form of defamation such as slander and libel, seditious speeches are outside the protection of this right.

2.2 The right to participate in every peaceful assembly to ventilate issues directly affecting the student’s welfare.

2.3 Through orderly procedure a student can appeal a grade he thinks was erroneously given to him.

2.4 A student can bring his grievance to the college dean regarding an unethical and unprofessional conduct of a faculty member:

2.4.1 Frequent absence and/or tardiness of a teacher from his classes.

2.4.2 Immoral advances, including sexual harassment, use of vulgar, insulting or derogatory words of any faculty member.

2.4.3 Coming to class unprepared to teach the lesson.

2.4.4 Smoking inside the classroom during class hours.

 3. Right to organize among themselves

4.1 Freedom to choose his field of study subject to existing curricula and to continue his course therein up to graduation, except in case of academic deficiency, or violation of disciplinary regulations and admission policies.

4.2 Freedom of inquiry and freedom to be in a liberal and democratic classroom environment where a student may air his own point of view regarding a specific discussion.

4.3 Freedom to propose to school authorities measures that could contribute best to the improvement of the academic atmosphere of the classroom.

4.4 Freedom to quality education, i.e. quality instruction, quality facilities and quality services.

 5. Right to elect his/her Club or Organization Officers.

 6. Right to know his/her grades.

 7. Right to see his/her corrected test papers, term papers and other class requirements.

 8. Right to the proper use of school facilities afforded to him/her.

 9. Right to effectively channel communications to appropriate academic and administrative bodies of the school, the right to promote and safeguard academic freedom, research freely and publish, discuss and exchange findings and recommendations for purposes of academic and social development.

 10. Right to due process, which includes the right to be informed in writing of the charge that may lead to any disciplinary action against him/her, the right to be heard and the right to present evidence in his/her favor.
