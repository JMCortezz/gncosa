DISCIPLINARY SANCTIONS

Why Rules and Regulations Matter?

Students are expected to abide by all school regulations. Rules and regulations promote a peaceful and disciplined learning environment, essential for personal growth and good citizenship.

The Deans and Director of Student Affairs handle external discipline matters. They investigate reported cases, decide on actions for minor infractions, and ensure a fair process for students facing misconduct allegations.

In more serious cases, a Disciplinary Committee is formed, including the Head of the Instruction Cluster as Chairman, the Director of Student Affairs, Deans, Adviser, and Legal Counsel. They apply due process, decide on actions, and the President's review makes them final with parental notification.

PROCEDURE FOR DISCIPLINARY ACTION

  • 1. Minor offenses committed by students are handled by the teacher and referred to the Academic Dean.
  • 2. For serious cases, the Office of Students Affairs is notified. They investigate all cases, ascertain facts, and impose the proper disciplinary sanctions.
  • 3. In the investigation of a serious case, due process is observed, including:

    a. A written report is submitted to the Director of Student Affairs stating the nature and circumstances of the offense.

    b. The Director of Student Affairs conducts the investigation or may call the Disciplinary Committee.

    c. Parents are duly notified by the Director of Student Affairs.

    d. If the student is found guilty, the Director of Student Affairs gives the disciplinary action.
