PENALTIES FOR SERIOUS CASES

Penalties for Serious Cases shall have the corresponding penalties Depending on the rightful recommendation of the Disciplinary Committee as approved by the President. Operation definition of the following penalties shall include the following:

Public Apology Depending on the gravity of the offense. The committee and the complainant shall agree on the enactment of this punishment.
Suspension A punishment for serious violation of the rules. A student on suspension will be required to stay home under the supervision of the parents or guardian during the period of his suspension. He is required to study for the classes he misses. The length of a student's suspension varies according to the gravity of the offense and the decision of the Disciplinary Committee.
Exclusion Exclusion Type A (the respondent is allowed to finish the semester/summer term and is allowed to apply a re-admission after at least one year) Exclusion Type B (the respondent is allowed to finish the semester/summer but is not allowed to re-enroll at the GNC) Exclusion Type C (the respondent is not allowed to finish the semester/summer and is not allowed to re-enroll at the GNC)
Dismissal serious penalty. A student who has been dismissed for serious offense from the GNC is never readmitted to GNC.
Expulsion Expulsion is an extreme form of administrative sanction which debars the student from all public and private schools. It requires the approval of the Department of Education/Commission of Higher Education.

Application for probation on discipline must be filed within fifteen (15) days after the final decision has been rendered.

The foregoing acts are not exclusive. Acts not governed by herein rules on discipline shall be governed by existing order or memoranda of the CHED, The Education Act of 1982 and the Manual of Information for Private Schools.

The Guagua National Colleges reserves the right to impose appropriate disciplinary actions against any student who is found after due investigation to have seriously violated the rules and regulations of the school.

Students who are found guilty after a series of investigation by the Disciplinary Committee shall be subjected to the following recommended penalties. The discretion of the Disciplinary Committee and the President shall be considered depending on the nature and gravity of the Offense.

HAZING BAN ON SORORITIES AND FRATERNITIES

Fraternities, sororities and societies, in any form are banned in the GNC at all levels. Any student who joins banned fraternities, sororities and societies or any unauthorized organization whether operating on or off campus shall be subjected to dismissal or expulsion.

Hazing or any act of physical violence as part thereof, done on or off campus is not allowed in all accredited or authorized student organizations. The officers of the organization shall be considered as guilty as the members of the organization directly involved in the hazing, or act of physical violence. The element of conspiracy shall be presumed and the same penalty of dismissal or expulsion on those who directly inflict the hazing or violence shall be meted out to.
